Agate

Agate is the name given to many
different types of banded Chalcedony,
a mineral from the Quartz family. Its
name is derived from the Achetes River
in Sicily, where Agates were first
discovered. Typically banded in layers
or stripes, some variants feature "eye"
marks or specks of color, some have
fossilized inclusions, and some are solid.
Agate, often known as the earth rainbow,
forms concentric bands in practically every
hue the earth can create, including a
colorless variety.

Agate has a lower intensity and vibrates
at a slower frequency than other stones,
but it is widely valued for its stabilizing
and strengthening properties.The stratified
bands of tiny quartz in Agate may appear
fragile, but they are actually extremely
robust. Agate is wonderful for balancing
emotional, physical, and intellectual
energies, as well as harmonizing yin and
yang, the universe's positive and negative
elements.

Agate encourages maturity, poise, and
inner stability.Its cozy, shielding
qualities promote safety and
self-assurance. Using this gem when
pregnant is highly recommended.
Agate jewelry placed between the breasts
promotes milk and helps new moms avoid
the "baby blues" that can occasionally
accompany childbirth.

Agate helps people balancing obligations
or many occupations by quelling the
burning desire for items we do not need.
It helps young toddlers learn to walk
and not fall when they get older,
and it helps authors convey ideas in a way
that is marketable. Agate is also
good for faithfulness in marriage.
When traveling, agate is a helpful
protective amulet that is particularly
good against road accidents.

Tree Agate

The fact that money is green is
not a coincidence. In actuality,
green hue has long been connected to
prosperity and fortune. The ancients
understood the critical need of oxygen
for life support when they observed the
abundance of life in full bloom among
the lush foliage of the great forests.
Green, which represents the complex
interplay between sun, water, and
photosynthesis, is a sign of development
and abundance, representing the earth
in its prime.

Within the realm
of gemstones, the vivid green hue of
the Tree Agate crystal is associated
with prosperity and fortunate
circumstances, rendering it perfect as
a guardian talisman against energy
vampires attempting to sap your vitality.
The defensive qualities of this
potent warrior stone chase away buzz
kills, dream crushers, and any other
negative forces that tell you that
you're not good enough, even your
own inner critic. According to Feng
Shui, it infuses your room with the
energy of nature without the maintenance
of a real plant, making it an ideal
option for people who aren't very
green. Allow the stone to serve as
a lovely reminder of your close
relationship with the great outdoors
and its abundance of natural wonders.
